Our Story

It started with my own daughter

I began ThinkRight with no certainty. My starting point was my own daughter, I wanted better for her brain than what existed. So I went looking into neuroscience, Japanese right-brain methodology, and developmental research that most educators in India had never encountered.

It started small. The doubts were real, and the early days were harder than I expected. Right-brain training wasn't a familiar concept for most Indian parents and we had to earn trust slowly, one family at a time. But we kept going.

Twelve years have passed. The journey is still tough. But 10,000 children across 11 centres have come through ThinkRight now. Every single one of them reminds me that the work is worth it.

We're still growing. We're still learning. We feel like we're just getting started.

Our Methodology

Four principles
that shape every session

ThinkRight isn't an opinion about how children learn, it's an architecture built from neuroscience and field-tested across 10,000+ kids.

01

The right brain first

The right brain is dominant in early childhood. It processes information through images, patterns, rhythm, and emotion. We meet the child's brain where it actually is stimulating it through the channels it is already wired to receive.

02

The 0–7 window

Neural plasticity is at its absolute peak before age 7. After this window, the brain doesn't stop learning but it will never again learn this fast, this easily, or with this level of permanence.

03

Joyful repetition

Research shows children need 10–15 exposures before a new concept enters long-term memory. Our curriculum is built around deliberate, joyful repetition, the same sequences revisited and layered across sessions until mastery is real and lasting.

What we build

Most programs tell you what they teach
We'd rather tell you what changes

Six outcomes that show up in every child who comes through ThinkRight in the classroom, on the sports field and in every room they walk into.

01

Stronger memory

Speed + accuracy + permanence.

Not cramming, not rote learning. Children develop the ability to absorb and recall information with a speed and accuracy that consistently surprises their teachers. We train visual memory, linking memory and sequence recall, the kind of memory that makes learning feel effortless.

02

Creative thinking

Lateral thinking and imagination.

We build the kind of creativity that helps a child approach a problem from an angle nobody else thought of. Lateral thinking, imagination, and the ability to make unexpected connections. These are right brain strengths that no exam tests but every successful person has.

03

Sharper focus

Attention as a trained skill.

In a world designed to distract, attention is everything. Our sessions build genuine, sustained focus, not through discipline, but through activities that train the brain's attention-regulation systems from the inside out.

04

Confidence

Trusts themselves, isn't afraid to try.

A child who trusts their memory, knows they can figure things out, and isn't afraid to try. It shows up in the classroom, on the sports field, and in every room they walk into.

05

Emotional intelligence

Self-awareness, empathy, regulation.

The inner skills that research consistently shows matter more than IQ for long-term success. We build these alongside cognitive development, not separately from it.

06

Love of learning

The one outcome we're proudest of.

A child who loves learning will always find a way forward. This is the result we are most proud of, the one that is hardest to measure but easiest to see.
